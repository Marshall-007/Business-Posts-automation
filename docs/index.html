Runs the posting workflow on GitHub. Choose a flow below, or run the standard post to every configured platform.

Schedule Instagram posts

Add up to three images or videos with a day/time for each, and choose Feed or Story. Media is uploaded to your repo, posted at the chosen time, then removed. Feed images are squared to 1080x1080; Story images are sized to 1080x1920. Feed videos post as Reels; Story videos post as Stories (MP4/MOV, under 40 MB). Stories vanish after 24 hours and ignore the caption. Requires the repo to be Public.

Content campaigns (hands-free posting)

Each campaign is a folder under content/ with its own start date. Inside it, day1/posts and day1/stories post on the start date; day2 the next day, and so on. Drop images or videos in and they post automatically, no uploading here. If a folder holds 2 files they go out 6 hours apart; 3 files 4 hours apart; 4+ files 3 hours apart (first at the start time). Images are converted and sized automatically (.webp/.png are fine; feed 1080x1080, story 1080x1920). Captions: a photo1.txt next to photo1.jpg, or one caption.txt for the folder, else auto-written; stories have no caption. Files are removed after posting. Queued items also appear in the Queue above and can be cancelled there.

Bulk upload and auto-sort

Select a pile of unsorted images and they are sorted into day folders for a campaign: feed images fill posts (2 or 3 per day), story images fill stories (2 or 3 per day), creating day1, day2, day3... as needed. Feed images are sized to 1080x1080, story images to 1080x1920, and everything is committed in one go. If the campaign name is new it is created. After uploading, set the campaign's start date and enable it.
